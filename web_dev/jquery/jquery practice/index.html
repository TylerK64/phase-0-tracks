Overview

This website will cover the following topics:

Setup

Before we begin, let's make sure you have the necessary equipment to learn all about drumming.

What you will need:
  • Pair of drumsticks
  • Flat surface to drum on
  • Metronome (optionally recommended)

5 Basic Essential Skills

Will our tools in hand, we can now go over 5 important skills every drummer should have.

  1. Tempo - The speed at which the the passage of music should be played. Often measured as beats per minute (BPM). Practice by keeping in time with your metronome to a specific BPM.
  2. Rudiments - Knowledge of drumming rudiments is critical for one's success. There are many different types that are commonly displayed in music. You can find more information at the following link.
  3. Traditional and Matched Grip - With traditional grip, the right stick is held straight forward like a wand while the left hand is held between the index and ring finger, like a fork. Matched grip is when each hand holds one stick straight forward, palms down like a wand.
  4. Reading Music - In order to play prearranged pieces, one must first be able to translate sheet music into its proper meaning.
  5. Types of Drums and Percussion - Knowing the different types of drums is crucial for any developing drummer. Drum kits typically include: a snare drum, bass drum, hi hat, a few toms, and a couple of cymbals. There are also many types of percussion instruments that can help maintain the groove of any song.
